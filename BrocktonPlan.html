Implementation Strategy

This section details the proposed strategy for implementing the AI coaching plan. It covers the phased approach to recordings, the coaching cycle, expected teacher participation, and key technical considerations for a smooth rollout.

Phased Approach ▼ Click to expand

Initial Recordings:

Math/Instructional Support Providers (MSPs) will initiate the recording process with teachers. This will help introduce the technology and process.

Teacher Recordings:

Following the initial MSP-led session, teachers will be encouraged to record themselves up to twice between each quarterly in-person coaching meeting. This aims for a manageable frequency, approximately once every two weeks.

Coaching Cycle ▼ Click to expand

Quarterly Sessions: In-person coaching sessions will occur on a quarterly basis.

Supplementary Data: Teacher-uploaded recordings will supplement these in-person meetings, providing ongoing data for coaching conversations.

Technical Considerations ▼ Click to expand

Feedback Filtering: Explore filtering feedback visibility by email suffix if needed.

Metric Development: Acknowledge the ongoing development of metrics like talk time and the challenge of identifying transitions between instructional modes. Brockton may serve as a receptive environment for piloting prototypes of these features.

Feedback Customization and Focus

This area describes how AI-generated feedback will be managed and tailored to Brockton's specific needs. It emphasizes control over feedback content, initial priority areas, and the importance of not overwhelming teachers.

Controlled Feedback:

The platform allows for control over what feedback is shared with teachers.

Brockton's Priorities:

  • Initially, focus feedback on areas like teacher talk time and wait time, as these are seen as potentially more impactful starting points than questioning.
  • While Brockton has a previous focus on questioning, the plan will start with talk time and wait time, with the option to customize and potentially isolate feedback related to questioning later if desired.

Avoiding Overwhelm:

The plan will prioritize meeting Brockton's specific needs and avoid overwhelming teachers with too much feedback.

Data Sharing with Administrators

This section explains the plan for sharing insights derived from the AI coaching with school administrators. It highlights Brockton's receptiveness to data-driven approaches and potential areas of interest for administrators.

Aggregated Insights: Aggregated data and insights from the AI coaching can be shared with school administrators.

Data-Driven Culture: This aligns with Brockton's receptiveness to data-driven insights, partly due to state directives.

Admin Interests: Data regarding time spent on independent work may also be of interest to administrators.
